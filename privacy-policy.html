Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Oak Consultancy collects, uses and protects personal information. We are committed to respecting your privacy and handling your data in a clear and transparent way.

Last updated: 9 December 2025

In short, here’s what this policy covers

What we collect

Basic contact details, project information and administrative details you share when you work with Oak Consultancy.

How we use it

To respond to enquiries, provide our services, manage contracts and keep straightforward records of organisations we support.

Your choices

You can ask to see, correct or delete your information, or object to how it’s used, in line with UK data protection law.

Who we are

Oak Consultancy provides funding, bid writing and strategic planning support to charities, community groups, schools, sports clubs and small businesses.

For the purposes of data protection law, Oak Consultancy is the “data controller” of the personal information described in this notice. If you have any questions about this policy, you can contact us at:

What information we collect

The information we collect depends on how you choose to engage with us. It may include:

  • Contact details such as your name, email address, phone number and the organisation you represent.
  • Project information that you share with us when we discuss your organisation, funding needs or plans.
  • Website enquiries – information you submit through our contact form or by emailing us directly.
  • Administrative and billing information where we are working with you under a contract.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (such as health information) through this website. If such information is relevant to a piece of work, we will discuss how it is handled with you separately.

How we use your information

We use personal information only where we have a lawful basis to do so, including:

  • To respond to enquiries and provide information about our services.
  • To deliver consultancy services you have requested and manage our relationship with you.
  • To issue invoices and maintain our financial records.
  • To keep basic records of organisations we work with for reference and planning.

We do not sell your personal data to anyone and we do not use it for large-scale marketing campaigns.

How we store and share your information

  • Storage: We store information in secure email, document and accounting systems. We keep information only for as long as it is reasonably needed for the purposes described in this policy.
  • Sharing: We may share limited personal information with trusted third parties who support our work (for example, IT or accounting providers), but only where this is necessary and subject to appropriate safeguards.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or to protect our legal rights.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you have a number of rights in relation to your personal information, including the right to:

  • Ask for a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Ask us to delete certain information, or to restrict how it is used, in some circumstances.
  • Object to our use of your information in some situations.

If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details above. We may need to verify your identity before responding to your request.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our services or legal obligations. The latest version will always be available on this page, and we will update the “Last updated” date at the top.
