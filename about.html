Our story

Oak Consultancy was founded by people who have seen first-hand how much time and energy community organisations pour into their work – and how frustrating it is when funding processes get in the way.

Over the years, we’ve worked with charities, schools, clubs and social enterprises that are delivering excellent work but are stretched thin. Staff and volunteers juggle front-line delivery, governance, safeguarding, facilities, reporting and everything in between. Funding applications and tenders are often squeezed into evenings, weekends or the margins of the day.

Oak Consultancy was set up to offer a different kind of support: practical, honest and tailored to the realities of smaller and mid-sized organisations, rather than assuming there is a dedicated fundraising or development team.

“We kept seeing brilliant work squeezed into the margins of the day just to keep the lights on. Oak exists so organisations can breathe, plan and grow without burning out.”

If this sounds familiar, you’re not alone

  • Big ambitions, but not enough time to chase every opportunity.
  • A mix of short-term grants, last-minute bids and occasional tenders.
  • Leaders carrying a lot of responsibility, often late into the evening.

What it’s like to work with Oak

We try to keep things as simple and grounded as possible. No big reveal, no mystery process – just clear steps that fit around your reality.

1. Start with a conversation

We begin by talking about what’s on your plate – upcoming deadlines, pressures, hopes and the things that keep getting pushed to the bottom of the list.

2. Agree where we can help

Together we decide where Oak can add most value. We’re upfront if we don’t think we’re the right fit or if a fund doesn’t look realistic.

3. Do the work alongside you

We draft, shape and strengthen bids, plans and tenders with you – keeping your voice and community knowledge at the centre.

4. Review and plan ahead

We reflect on what’s been learned, how funders have responded and how to make the next round of bids simpler and less stressful.

Our approach

These are the things people notice most when they work with us.

Plain language, not jargon

We explain processes and funding requirements in clear terms, so you always know what’s happening and why – and can explain it easily to your board or committee.

Realistic, not idealistic

We work with the time and capacity you have, not an imaginary full team that doesn’t exist. Plans are designed to be lived with, not just presented once.

Honest conversations

If something isn’t realistic, or a fund isn’t a good fit, we’ll say so and explain why. We’d rather protect your energy than chase every opportunity on paper.

Long-term thinking

We help you build a pipeline and a plan, not just chase one-off pots of money. The aim is to give your organisation a steadier footing over time.

Above all, we aim to be a calm, reliable presence alongside your organisation – someone you can talk things through with, ask questions of and lean on when deadlines or decisions feel heavy.

What we value

A few principles underpin how we work with every organisation.

Clarity

We prioritise clear communication, straightforward documents and practical next steps. You should never be left wondering what happens next.

In practice: after a session, boards know what’s been agreed, who’s doing what and when decisions are due.

Integrity

We will only encourage bids and tenders that feel appropriate and realistic for your organisation. If we don’t think something is a good idea, we will explain our reasoning.

In practice: sometimes that means advising against an opportunity – even if it means less work for us.

Partnership

We see our work as a partnership, not a one-way service. Your knowledge of your community or sector, combined with our experience of funding and planning, is what makes the work effective.

In practice: we bring structure and language; you bring lived experience and local insight. We build everything from there, together.
