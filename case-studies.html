Case studies from organisations like yours.

The examples below are based on real patterns of work with community organisations, schools and clubs. Names and details are anonymised, but the challenges and outcomes will feel familiar to many of the groups Oak Consultancy supports.

How to read these case studies

Each case study follows the same structure: the situation an organisation was facing, the support Oak Consultancy provided, and the outcomes they achieved.

  • Situation: Where the organisation was starting from and what felt difficult.
  • Support: The practical steps we took together.
  • Outcome: The difference it made in terms of funding, capacity or confidence.

If you recognise your own organisation in any of these examples, we can talk about how a similar approach might work in your context.

Community centre increasing capacity through an improved bid pipeline

Situation

A busy community centre was delivering a wide range of activities: older people’s groups, youth sessions, wellbeing classes and a small advice service. Funding was a patchwork of short-term grants, and staff were writing bids late at night or between sessions. There was no clear view of upcoming opportunities or how they linked to the centre’s priorities.

Support from Oak Consultancy

  • Initial mapping of existing projects, costs and current funding sources.
  • Facilitated conversation with staff and trustees to define 3–4 core priorities for the next 18–24 months.
  • Creation of a simple funding pipeline, identifying realistic grants for each priority.
  • Ongoing bid writing support for key opportunities, including drafting, budgeting and submission checks.

Outcomes

  • Several successful grants, including a multi-year award that stabilised core staffing.
  • Clearer sense of which opportunities to pursue and which to leave, reducing pressure on staff.
  • A simple, shared plan that trustees could use to monitor progress and support decisions.

Small sports club securing multi-year youth development funding

Situation

A small sports club was running popular youth sessions but relying on annual fundraising events and a few small grants. Volunteers wanted to expand their programme and reduce participation costs for families, but weren’t sure where to look for larger, multi-year funding.

Support from Oak Consultancy

  • Clarification of the club’s aims for youth development over the next three years.
  • Support to cost out session delivery, coaching, equipment and volunteer training.
  • Identification of suitable sport and youth-focused funders with multi-year grants.
  • Drafting of a compelling bid that linked sport participation to wider outcomes (confidence, wellbeing, inclusion).

Outcomes

  • Secured a multi-year grant to support coaching hours, equipment and targeted outreach.
  • Reduced pressure on volunteers to continually organise small fundraising events.
  • Stronger narrative about the club’s wider impact, which could be reused in future bids and communications.

School PTA accessing grant support for playground improvements

Situation

A primary school PTA wanted to improve outdoor play spaces, particularly for children with additional needs. They had enthusiasm and some fundraising experience, but limited time to research grants or prepare detailed applications alongside busy jobs and family commitments.

Support from Oak Consultancy

  • Discussion with school leadership and PTA members to clarify needs and priorities.
  • Support to gather quotes and outline a phased plan for playground improvements.
  • Identification of relevant funders with an interest in children, inclusion and outdoor learning.
  • Bid writing support, including drafting the narrative, outcomes and budget breakdown.

Outcomes

  • Successful grant funding towards the first phase of playground improvements.
  • A clear case for support and costed plan that could be used for future applications.
  • Increased confidence among PTA members about approaching funders and explaining their project.
