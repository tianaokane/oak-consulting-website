Bid writing that speaks funders’ language and reflects your reality.

Many organisations are doing excellent work but struggle to find time—and the right words—to explain it to funders. Oak Consultancy helps you identify realistic funding opportunities and prepare clear, honest bids that show the difference you make.

Who is our bid writing support for?

This service is a good fit if you are:

  • A charity, community group, school, sports club, church, social enterprise or CIC.
  • Delivering work that clearly benefits people or communities.
  • Short on time or confidence when it comes to writing applications.
  • Frustrated by “almost there” or repeated unsuccessful bids.

You don’t need to have a perfect project plan written up. If you have a rough idea of what you want to do, we can help you shape it into something funders can understand and support.

What’s included in our bid writing support

The exact support depends on your package and needs, but typically includes:

1. Funding search & fit

  • Identifying realistic grants and funds for your size, location and type of work.
  • Checking eligibility and guidance so time is not wasted on poor fits.
  • Discussing which opportunities to prioritise based on capacity and timelines.

2. Shaping your project

  • Clarifying what you want to deliver, who will benefit and what will change as a result.
  • Helping you define outcomes, activities and a realistic delivery plan.
  • Advising on how to describe existing work in a clear, structured way.

3. Writing and editing bids

  • Drafting responses to application questions in funder-friendly language.
  • Working with you to ensure bids still sound like your organisation, not generic text.
  • Preparing supporting information (case studies, evidence, policies) where needed.

4. Budgets & supporting documents

  • Helping you build clear, realistic budgets that match your plans.
  • Checking that figures are consistent across the application and attachments.
  • Advising on monitoring and evaluation information funders often require.

How our bid writing process works

We keep the process straightforward and as light on your time as possible.

  1. Initial conversation. We talk through your organisation, current work and what you’d like to fund. You can also share any previous bids and feedback.
  2. Agree focus and priorities. Together we decide which bid(s) to tackle first, based on deadlines, fit and capacity.
  3. Information gathering. We request key documents and details (e.g. budgets, project ideas, existing reports) so we can write accurately.
  4. Drafting and review. Oak Consultancy prepares a draft bid. You review, correct and add detail where needed; we then refine and finalise.
  5. Submission. We support you to submit on time, ensuring the application and any attachments are complete and consistent.
  6. Learning and follow-up. Where funders provide feedback, we review it with you and use it to strengthen future bids.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance do we need to contact you?

Ideally, several weeks before the deadline so there is time to shape the project, gather information and go through at least one round of review. Tight deadlines may still be possible, but options can be more limited.

Can you guarantee a successful outcome?

No consultant can guarantee funding, as decisions ultimately sit with funders. What we can do is ensure your bids are clear, eligible, realistic and well-presented, and help you learn from any unsuccessful attempts.

Do we have to commit to a full package?

Not necessarily. While many organisations prefer a package for ongoing support, we can also discuss one-off pieces of work for specific bids or time-limited projects.

What information will you need from us?

Typically we’ll ask for basic organisational information, project ideas, any existing plans or reports, recent accounts and a sense of your current capacity. We’ll keep the requests as focused and manageable as we can.

Want help with an upcoming bid?

If you already have a fund or deadline in mind, or you simply want to explore what’s possible, a short conversation is the best place to start.
